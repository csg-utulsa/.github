## Cinematic Artist / Sequencer

Cinematic artists use Unity Timeline or Unreal Sequencer to choreograph cameras, animation, lighting, VFX, and audio into polished real-time cutscenes and scripted moments that support story, mood, and gameplay.

> \[!IMPORTANT\]
> 
> ### What Your Portfolio Should Contain
> 
> -   **Storyboards / Animatics** (planned shots + pacing before 3D)
> -   **Sequencer/Timeline Cinematics** (30â€“60 second in-engine sequences)
> -   **Camera Direction Proof** (shot composition, lens choices, camera movement)
> -   **Environmental Motion + VFX** (timed props, particles, lighting changes)
> -   **Gameplay-to-Cinematic Integration** (triggered sequences + transitions)
> -   **Audio + Timing Sync** (cuts, motion, and events aligned to sound/music)
> -   **Final Reel / Presentation Clips** (clean edited output with captions)
>     

# Portfolio Deliverables Checklist 
Each cinematic sequence in your portfolio should demonstrate your ability to plan, direct, and implement real-time scenes, with clear evidence of timing, shot design, and technical sequencing.
-   **Required Deliverables:** Must be included for every cinematic piece.
-   **Add-On Deliverables:** Include only if you personally created them (audio, VFX, scripting, etc.).
-   If you collaborated, clearly indicate which parts you were responsible for.
