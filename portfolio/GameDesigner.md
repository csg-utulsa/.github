# Game Designer
Game Designers are the **architects of player experience**, defining ‚Äúfun‚Äù through **mechanics, rules, and system interactions**. Their work ensures a game is **playable, engaging, balanced, and rewarding**, from the first minute to the last. Unlike artists, designers must make **abstract systems and experiences visible** through documentation, diagrams, prototypes, and playtest evidence.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> 
> ### What Your Portfolio Should Contain
>
> -   **Game Design Documents (GDDs / One-Pagers):** Clearly describe vision, systems, and mechanics.
> -   **System & Mechanics Breakdowns:** How features interact and affect player experience.
> -   **Gameplay Loops & Core Loops:** Visualizations of action, reward, and feedback cycles.
> -   **Balance & Economy Data:** Spreadsheets or charts showing stats, probability, and progression.
> -   **Iterative Design Evidence:** Notes and changes based on playtesting or player data.
> -   **Playable Demos or Gameplay Footage:** Demonstrate how mechanics function in practice.
>     

# Portfolio Deliverables Checklist
Each portfolio piece should **demonstrate your ability to design, refine, and balance systems and player experiences**, supported by documentation, diagrams, and playable examples.
-   **Required Deliverables:** Must be included for every portfolio piece.
-   **Add-On Deliverables:** Include if you personally contributed (balance analysis, pacing charts, iteration logs, etc.).
-   If you collaborated, clearly indicate which parts you were responsible for.

## Add-On Deliverables
### D) Systems & Balance (The Math Side)
- [ ] **Balance Spreadsheet:** Enemy stats, XP curves, weapon stats, or probability tables.
- [ ] **Probability & Economy Charts:** Drop rates, progression curves, or TTK (time-to-kill) metrics.
- [ ] **Live-Ops / Retention Plan (Optional):** How the game stays engaging over time (daily quests, battle passes).
    
### E) Mission & Encounter Design (The Pacing Side)
- [ ] **Top-Down Mission Map:** 2D layout showing encounter beats, safe zones, objectives, and player flow.
- [ ] **Beat Sheet / Pacing Curve:** Graph of intensity or engagement throughout the level/mission.
- [ ] **Objective Logic / Triggers:** Notes on how game events are triggered by player actions.
    
### F) Player Research & Playtesting
- [ ]  **Player Personas:** Target player profiles with motivations, playstyles, and behaviors.
- [ ] **Playtest Data Visualization:** Feedback summaries, heatmaps, or survey results.
- [ ]  **Actionable Changes:** Documented design decisions made in response to playtesting.

### G) Documentation Quality (Professional Standards)
- [ ] **Formatting & Readability:** Clear GDDs with headings, bullet points, and easy navigation.
- [ ] **Version Control / Changelog:** History of document evolution throughout development.
