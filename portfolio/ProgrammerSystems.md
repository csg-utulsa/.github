# Programmer â€“ Game Systems

Game systems programmers focus on building robust, scalable, and maintainable architecture. This includes AI, physics, UI systems, networking, multiplayer functionality, performance optimization, and custom tool or engine development. Their work ensures the game runs smoothly, systems are maintainable, and gameplay features integrate reliably with the underlying architecture.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> ### What Your Portfolio Should Contain
> - **Modular & Extensible Systems** (inventory, quest systems, ability frameworks, save/load)
> - **Architectural Design Patterns** (Factories, Observers, Strategy)
> - **AI & Decision Logic** (Behavior Trees, GOAP, Utility AI, pathfinding optimization)
> - **Data-Driven Design** (JSON, ScriptableObjects, Databases to drive content)
> - **Performance & Profiling** (CPU/GPU optimization, memory management, multi-threading)
> - **Networking & Persistence** (state synchronization, multiplayer prototypes, serialization)
> - **Integration Examples** (working with art, audio, UI, and gameplay features)
> - **Clean, SOLID, Documented Code** (production-ready, properly commented, readable, and maintainable)
>

# Portfolio Deliverables Checklist
Each portfolio piece should demonstrate your ability to **design, implement, and optimize complex systems** that integrate with other teams while maintaining clarity, performance, and scalability.
-   **Required Deliverables:** Must be included for every system portfolio piece.
-   **Add-On Deliverables:** Include only if you personally created them (AI, physics, networking, tools, etc.).
-   If you collaborated, clearly indicate which parts were your responsibility.
